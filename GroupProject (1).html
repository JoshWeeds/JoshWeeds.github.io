Introduction¶

The purpose of this model is to walk users through the data science pipeline. Our group decided to use the Campaign Finance Data from the Federal Election Committee. The reason why we decided to go with this dataset is because we would like to see if contributions toward each political party will have impact on democracy. Campaign finance is a crucial part of modern democratic systems, and analyzing the flow of money in elections allows us to understand political power dynamics. The FEC dataset can show how money influences elections, policy decisions, and who gets elected. This makes it highly relevant for anyone interested in political science, public policy, or the intersection of money and politics.

Three (potential) questions posed:

  1. Does a shorter first name lead to an increase in disbursements?
  2. Is there is a difference in the significance of the total disimbursements and political party that the disimbursements were contributed to?
  3. Do donations in non-election years predict success during elections?

We are answering those questions because it can shed light on the impact of money on the political process, which is central to understanding democracy and fairness in the system. Additioanlly, answering these questions can help assess whether some groups or individuals have more influence than others due to their financial contributions, which speaks to issues of equity and access in the political process. Finally, by identifying patterns and trends in campaign finance, you can predict how future elections might be shaped by money. This is valuable for both political analysts and future candidates seeking to understand where they should focus their fundraising efforts.

def trim_results(df, year):
    df_trimmed = df[['STATE ABBREVIATION', 'CANDIDATE NAME', 'GE WINNER INDICATOR']]
    df_trimmed.replace("NaN", pd.NA, inplace=True)
    df_trimmed['GE WINNER INDICATOR'].fillna('L', inplace=True)
    #df_trimmed = df_trimmed.dropna()
    df_trimmed.rename(columns={
        'STATE ABBREVIATION': 'state',
        'CANDIDATE NAME': 'name',
        'GE WINNER INDICATOR': 'winner'
    }, inplace=True)
    df_trimmed.dropna(subset=['name'], inplace=True)
    df_trimmed = df_trimmed[df_trimmed['name'] != 'Scattered']
    df_trimmed['cycle'] = year
    df_trimmed.reset_index(drop=True, inplace=True)
    # Capitalize 'name' column
    df_trimmed['name'] = df_trimmed['name'].str.upper()
    return df_trimmed
